# 📊 Council Financial Performance Analysis (2024–2025)

This project evaluates the financial performance of six councils using structured reporting, comparative metrics, and operational analysis.

The objective is straightforward:

- Compare profitability across councils  
- Assess financial sustainability (with and without grants)  
- Identify structural risks and efficiency opportunities  

---

# 📁 Report Scope

**Councils Analyzed:**

- The Hills Shire  
- City of Sydney  
- City of Parramatta  
- Hornsby Shire  
- North Sydney  
- Woollahra Municipal  

**Core Financial Metrics:**

- Operating Margin % (profitability per $1 earned)  
- Net Operating Result  
- Net Result (Before Grants)  
- Grants Dependency %  
- Own-Source Revenue %  
- Employee Cost % of Expenses  

---

# 📌 Executive Summary

This year’s results show major differences in profitability, efficiency, and funding models.

## 🥇 The Hills Shire — Efficiency Leader

- **Operating Margin:** 47.0%  
- **Net Result (Before Grants):** +$29,210  
- **Grant Dependency:** 44.6%  

The Hills keeps 47 cents of every dollar earned. Even without grants, core operations remain profitable.

This is the benchmark council.

---

## 🏙 City of Sydney — Largest Scale, Moderate Efficiency

- **Total Income:** $909,748  
- **Operating Margin:** 14.7%  
- **Own-Source Revenue:** 86.0%  
- **Profit Change YoY:** -$14.5k  
- **Beat Budget By:** +$18.1k  

Sydney generates the most revenue and is highly self-reliant. Profitability is solid but not exceptional given its scale.

The opportunity here is operational efficiency, not revenue growth.

---

## ⚠️ City of Parramatta — Emerging Financial Risk

- **Operating Margin:** 12.7%  
- **Budget Variance:** -$25.9k  
- **Profit Change YoY:** -$32.9k  
- **Net Result (Before Grants):** -$13.5k  

Core operations are running at a loss.

The only reason Parramatta remained profitable overall was reliance on capital grants. That is not sustainable.

Primary issue:
- Sharp drop in “Other Income” (-$64.7k)
- Rising depreciation costs (-$8.5k)
- Staff costs at 43.0% of total spending

This council needs cost discipline and more stable revenue.

---

## 📈 Hornsby Shire — Most Improved

- **Profit Increase YoY:** +$90.5k  
- Gains driven by:
  - Higher rates revenue (+$9.4k)  
  - Lower materials spending (+$10.0k)  
  - Minor rise in staff costs (-$4.4k)  

Hornsby improved across multiple levers. Financial position is stable and strengthening.

---

# 💰 Income & Funding Structure

Councils rely on grants very differently:

| Council | Grant Dependency |
|----------|----------------|
| The Hills | 44.6% |
| City of Sydney | 14.0% |
| Woollahra | 9.5% |

Sydney is highly self-funded.  
The Hills is profitable but more exposed to grant shifts.

Different funding models carry different risks.

---

# 🧾 Expense Structure

Two clear operating models emerge:

## 1️⃣ “People-Heavy” Model
- City of Parramatta (43.0% staff costs)
- City of Sydney (39.5%)

Higher reliance on internal workforce.

## 2️⃣ “Balanced” Model
- The Hills  
- Hornsby  
- North Sydney  
- Woollahra  

More even distribution between staffing and materials/services.

The model choice impacts flexibility during revenue pressure.

---

# 🎯 Key Performance Insights

## 1. The Hills Shire is the benchmark
Strong surplus management and profitable core services.

## 2. Sydney has scale but average efficiency
High revenue does not automatically translate to high margins.

## 3. Parramatta is exposed
Grant reliance and declining core income create financial risk.

## 4. Hornsby executed well
Clear year-over-year improvement through cost management.

## 5. Operating model matters
Staff-heavy councils are more vulnerable when revenue dips.

---

# 🚀 Strategic Recommendations

## For City of Sydney
- Improve operational efficiency
- Optimize workforce cost structure

## For City of Parramatta
- Stabilize recurring income streams
- Reduce exposure to volatile “Other Income”
- Control staff and depreciation growth

## For The Hills Shire
- Maintain surplus discipline
- Reduce long-term grant dependency risk

## For Hornsby
- Continue cost optimization strategy
- Protect rate-based income growth

---

# 📌 Conclusion

The six councils are in very different financial positions.

- **The Hills Shire** leads in profitability and operational strength.  
- **City of Sydney** dominates in scale and self-reliance but has efficiency headroom.  
- **City of Parramatta** shows early warning signs and must correct course.  
- **Hornsby Shire** demonstrated strong execution and improvement.  

What this really means is simple:

Financial sustainability is driven by three levers:

- Core operational profitability  
- Cost discipline  
- Revenue stability independent of grants  

Councils that manage all three win long term.
